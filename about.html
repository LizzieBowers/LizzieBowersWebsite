About Me

How I Became Dizzy

When I was 20 years old, life had an unexpected turn that led to me living at home again. There were some positive things about the situation, such as time to develop a better relationship with my little brother and time for healing before figuring the rest of my life out. During this time, I resurrected an old nickname for my brother that I’d given him as a baby, Drewble, and he started to look for a new one for me.

During this time, I started taking a medication that, as a side effect, makes me dizzy. It was not the most comfortable for me because I rarely get dizzy, and when I do it’s not for long. My brother started calling me Dizzy, and I rejected it. But then I realized that I loved that he came up with it. And thus the nickname was born.

After my life had some time to sort itself out, I went back to school and in the process discovered that dizzy is probably an apt descriptor for my life. In the middle of all of my messes and improbable circumstances, I’ve found time to be opinionated and or laugh. I’ve learned a lot from the diverse circumstances I’ve been in, and this is my way of sharing.
